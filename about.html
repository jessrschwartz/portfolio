About Me

Portrait Placeholder

Hi, I’m Jessica

I’m a web development student at San Diego Mesa College with a strong interest in front-end development, user experience design, and accessibility. I enjoy building interfaces that are visually engaging, thoughtfully structured, and usable by as many people as possible.

My background combines technical coursework with creative problem-solving. I’m especially drawn to projects that require balancing aesthetics, usability, and accessibility—making sure the experience works just as well with a keyboard or screen reader as it does with a mouse.

Design Philosophy

I believe accessibility and good design go hand in hand. When accessibility is built into a project from the start, the result is a cleaner, more intuitive experience for everyone.

I approach web development with an emphasis on semantic HTML, responsive layouts, and thoughtful interaction design. I’m intentional about how content is structured, how users move through a page, and how different users might interact with the same interface in different ways.

Skills & Focus Areas

  • HTML5 & CSS3 (semantic, responsive layouts)
  • JavaScript & jQuery fundamentals
  • UX / UI Design & Wireframing
  • Accessibility (WCAG 2, keyboard navigation, screen reader considerations)
  • GitHub & version control basics

Looking Ahead

As I continue developing my skills, I’m excited to take on projects that challenge me to think more deeply about inclusive design and real-world user needs. I’m especially interested in opportunities where I can grow as a front-end developer while contributing to thoughtful, accessible digital experiences.
